Socioeconomic Inequality
Growing up, I've lived in multiple places in the country, and the different socioeconomic disparity amongst the places is startling. Especially now that I teach in a low-income community, I'm even more interested in this inequality. And this is what I wanted to illustrate in the visualization: the disparity amongst the different regions of the country. I also wanted to know if this inequaltiy has always persisted, or has it gotten better or worse recently.
Graphed below is the Gini Coefficient, which represents the income distribution of a region's residents. The historical data came from here, and the recent Gini breakdown for each state is from Wikipedia.
I knew that the graphic would be small because I wanted to depict all 50 states plus Washington DC, so I had to consider how to make the illustration small and concise. For the historical data, I used an area graph because it can show the fluctuations in income disparity over time most clearly at a microscopic scale. For the current Gini breakdown, I knew that I wanted to see the trends across different states very quickly and a bar graph communicates this the easiest.
